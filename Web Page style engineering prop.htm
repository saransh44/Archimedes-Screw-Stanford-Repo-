I propose a solution to threatening problem of soil erosion due to constructions and renovations.  With all renovations comes a fair bit of digging underground. When construction workers dig about a half a story underground, there is good soil that we unfortunately have to build on. Most of the time there are pockets of murky water that has healthy soil with moisture, nutrients, and microbial life trapped inside. It would make sense to water the surrounding topsoil that had been eroded due to the construction around it with this new underground soil.    A device first theorized by Nebuchadnezzar II, the king of the Neo-Babylonian Empire with a little tweaking can be used today to meet this very need. He thought  of the conventional metal screw has thread running helically along its axis but enlarging this thread, hollowing it out, putting a wall around it, we could make for a passage of water upwards.

            Furthermore, by adding a small plastic ramp to guide the water down after coming up, we would automate watering aspect of the previously eroded soil. Using kinetic energy from a small DC motor, it would be possible to push water up the device with very little effort due to the water's relatively low weight which would cause negligible stress on the motor. On top of this the relatively high torque VEX motors will have to exert centripetal force which can easily structurally support 50 pounds with right gear ratio. As for the device and slide itself, it would be 3-D printed using grid based plastic style with flexible 3-D printing filament, allowing for changes in the general size of the product. The big differentiator of this liquid irrigation system is the very low amount of ghost space that is being used. Ghost space is the total amount space that is utilized when device is turned on and moving. The low amount of ghost space means that device could be put to full use in many of the cramped spaces that water hides in. Additionally, the flexible filament would allow for even a wider range of construction situations to use the irrigation device.

           

 

Compared to other irrigation systems, there is a low energy consumption. I would like to ask for a 5 million dollar investment into the idea.
